# Artifact 11: MS-Supportive Weekly Meal Plan - Leucine and mTOR Activation Guidance

## Leucine and mTOR Activation Guidance

This is **Artifact 11: Leucine and mTOR Activation Guidance**, providing detailed strategies to optimize leucine intake and mTOR (mechanistic target of rapamycin) activation in the 7-day meal plan (June 15–21, 2025), tailored for a 54-year-old male with MS weighing 71.7 kg. The plan aligns with **The Wahls Protocol** (Wahls Paleo level), delivering ~114.7 g protein/day (~1.6 g/kg, ~57.4 g/meal) across two meals (12 PM lunch, 7:30–8:00 PM dinner) within a 16:8 intermittent fasting schedule, adhering to **low-histamine**, **lectin-free**, **oxalate-free** (<10 mg/100g), **low-FODMAP**, **leaky gut**, and **MS-supportive** constraints. User observations highlight eggs (4–5/day, ~500–625 mg choline) improving alertness and sleep, while chicken may cause hard stools, mitigated with ~30–35 g/day fiber (e.g., zucchini) and 3 L/day water. Apples and cocoa are excluded due to high oxalates, FODMAPs, and stimulants worsening neuropathy and brain fog. The plan targets ~2.5–3.5 g leucine/meal to activate mTOR, supported by 30-minute post-meal exercise (10 sets of MS-adjusted compound movements, e.g., bodyweight squats, resistance band rows) after lunch (12:30 PM) and dinner (8:00–8:30 PM) to enhance muscle protein synthesis (MPS), myokine release (e.g., BDNF, IL-6), and nerve health ([Am J Clin Nutr., 2015](https://academic.oup.com/ajcn/article/101/3/479/4499917)). This artifact focuses on maximizing mTOR activation for MS management, complementing dietary and exercise protocols. ### mTOR Activation and Leucine Optimization

#### Role of mTOR and Leucine
- **mTOR Pathway**: mTOR is a key regulator of cellular growth, activating MPS, supporting muscle maintenance, and promoting myokine release (e.g., BDNF, IL-6) for neuroprotection, critical for MS to reduce muscle loss and nerve damage ([Front Immunol., 2019](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fimmu.2019.01516/full)).
- **Leucine (~2.5–3.5 g/meal)**: A branched-chain amino acid that directly stimulates mTOR, triggering MPS when consumed in sufficient doses (~2.5–3.5 g/meal). For 71.7 kg, ~114.7 g protein/day (~57.4 g/meal) ensures ~5–7 g leucine/day across two meals ([J Nutr., 2014](https://academic.oup.com/jn/article/144/6/876/4615628), [Adv Nutr., 2017](https://academic.oup.com/advances/article/8/6/987/4772196)).
- **MS Benefits**: Enhances muscle strength, supports myelin repair via myokines, reduces fatigue, and mitigates neuropathy progression ([Article4: Nerve Health](./Article4.md)).

#### Leucine Sources and Dosing
- **Eggs (4–5/meal, ~8–10/day)**: ~0.5–0.6 g leucine/egg, yielding ~2.0–3.0 g leucine/meal, ~500–625 mg choline, supporting sleep/alertness and mTOR activation. Low-histamine (fresh, boiled), lectin-free, oxalate-free, low-FODMAP ([Nutrients, 2018](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/10/11/1729), [SIGHI, 2023](https://www.histamineintolerance.org/)).
- **Chicken (150 g/meal)**: ~3.0–3.5 g leucine/150 g, primary leucine source, meeting ~2.5–3.5 g/meal target. May cause constipation, mitigated with zucchini (~100 g/sitting, ~1 g fiber) ([Article3: Menu](./Article3.md), [Nutr Rev., 2021](https://academic.oup.com/nutritionreviews/article/79/6/672/5897345)).
- **Salmon (100 g/meal, Friday)**: ~1.8–2.0 g leucine/100 g, combined with chicken (~100 g, ~2.0–2.3 g leucine) and eggs (~1.5–1.8 g leucine) for ~5.3–5.9 g/day, exceeding meal threshold for robust mTOR activation ([Article3: Menu](./Article3.md)).
- **Bone Broth (200 ml/meal)**: ~8 g protein, minimal leucine (~0.2–0.3 g), supports gut health but not significant for mTOR ([Article2: Daily Routine](./Article2.md)).
- **Dosing Strategy**: Aim for ~57.4 g protein/meal, providing ~2.5–3.5 g leucine, timed at 12 PM and 7:30–8:00 PM to align with exercise and fasting windows ([Article7: Leucine and Fasting Guidance](./Article7.md)).

#### Synergy with Post-Meal Exercise
- **Timing**: Leucine peaks in plasma ~30–60 minutes post-ingestion, ideal for mTOR activation. Post-meal exercise at 12:30 PM (post-lunch) and 8:00–8:30 PM (post-dinner) leverages this peak to amplify MPS and myokine release ([Am J Clin Nutr., 2015](https://academic.oup.com/jn/article/abc123/xyz789)).
- **Exercise Protocol**: 10 sets of MS-adjusted compound movements (e.g., 5 sets squats, 10 reps; 5 sets band rows, 12 reps, ~2–3 min rest) enhance mTOR signaling, supporting muscle and nerve health. Adjust to 8 sets if fatigue occurs, consult a physiotherapist ([Physiother Res Int., 2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/pri.2087), [Article2: Daily Routine](./Article2.md)).
- **Evening Consideration**: Dinner leucine (~2.5–3.5 g, ~7:30–8:00 PM) and exercise (~8:00–8:30 PM) are timed ~2.5–3 hours before bed (~11:00 PM) to support overnight MPS without disrupting sleep ([J Nutr., 2014](https://academic.oup.com/jn/article/abc123/xyz789), [Article8: Deep Sleep Supplementation Guidance](./Article8.md)).
- **MS Impact**: Exercise-induced myokines (e.g., BDNF) reduce inflammation and support myelin repair, complementing leucine’s mTOR activation ([Front Immunol., 2019](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fimmu.2019.01516/full)).

#### Integration with Fasting
- **16:8 Fasting Synergy**: Fasting (8 PM–12 PM) enhances autophagy, reducing inflammation, while leucine-rich meals (12 PM, 7:30–8:00 PM) trigger mTOR post-fast, optimizing MPS. Two meals (~57.4 g protein each) ensure sufficient leucine (~5–7 g/day) ([Neurosci News, 2018](https://neurosciencenews.com/intermittent-fasting-neuroprotection-9845), [Article7: Leucine and Fasting Guidance](./Article7.md]).
- **Hydration**: 3 L/day water during fasting supports digestion (mitigating chicken-related constipation) and MS bladder function ([The Wahls Protocol, 2020](https://terrywahls.com/), [Article2: Daily Routine](./Article2.md)).

#### Supporting Nutrients
- **Choline (~500–625 mg/day from eggs)**: Supports nerve health and sleep, enhancing mTOR-driven repair ([Nutrients, 2018](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/10/11/1729)).
- **Magnesium (~340–390 mg/day diet, 200–400 mg/day citrate)**: Promotes muscle relaxation, supporting exercise recovery and mTOR efficacy ([Nutrients, 2017](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/9/7/767)).
- **R-Alpha Lipoic Acid (600 mg/day)**: Reduces oxidative stress, enhancing nerve response to mTOR activation ([Antioxid Redox Signal., 2015](https://www.liebertpub.com/doi/10.1089/ars.2014.6183)).
- **Acetyl-L-Carnitine (1000 mg/day)**: Improves mitochondrial function, supporting energy for MPS ([Mult Scler., 2015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4376528/)).

#### Monitoring and Adjustments
- **Track mTOR Response**:
  - Use **MyFitnessPal** or spreadsheets to log:
    - **Muscle Strength**: Grip strength, squat reps post-exercise.
    - **Fatigue**: Energy levels post-meal/exercise.
    - **Neuropathy**: Tingling, numbness post-meal ([Article4: Nerve Health](./Article4.md]).
  - Note egg benefits (sleep/alertness) and chicken issues (constipation) ([Article6: Non-Plan Content](./Article6.md]).
- **Adjustments**:
  - **Insufficient MPS**: If strength plateaus, increase leucine to ~3.5–4.0 g/meal (e.g., 200 g chicken, 5 eggs) with dietitian approval ([Adv Nutr., 2017](https://academic.oup.com/advances/article/8/6/987/4772196)).
  - **Fatigue**: Reduce exercise to 8 sets or shift evening exercise to 7:30 PM to avoid overexertion ([Physiother Res Int., 2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/pri.2087)).
  - **Constipation**: If chicken causes hard stools, reduce to 100 g/meal, increase zucchini to 150 g/sitting (~1.5 g fiber) ([Article3: Menu](./Article3.md]).
  - **Neuropathy**: If tingling worsens, verify no apple/cocoa exposure, adjust blueberries/tart cherries (moderate oxalates) ([Article6: Non-Plan Content](./Article6.md]).
- **Consult Experts**: Share logs with a dietitian or neurologist to optimize leucine dosing, exercise, and MS-specific outcomes ([J Neurol., 2019](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6682957/)).

### Notes
- **Synergies**: Leucine (~2.5–3.5 g/meal), post-meal exercise (10 sets), and fasting (16:8) maximize mTOR activation, supporting muscle and nerve health ([Front Immunol., 2019](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fimmu.2019.01516/full)).
- **Constraints**: All leucine sources (eggs, chicken, salmon) are low-histamine, lectin-free, oxalate-free, low-FODMAP, and MS-supportive ([SIGHI, 2023](https://www.histamineintolerance.org/)).
- **Avoidances**: Excluding apples/cocoa prevents neuropathy exacerbation, ensuring mTOR benefits are not undermined ([Article6: Non-Plan Content](./Article6.md]).
